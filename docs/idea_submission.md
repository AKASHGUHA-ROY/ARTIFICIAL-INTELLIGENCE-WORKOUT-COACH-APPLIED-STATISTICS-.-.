## Idea Submission — AI Workout Trainer (MVP)

### Objective (no jargon)
Our aim for the Minimum Viable Product project is to build an Artificial Intelligence workout trainer that provides **live session guidance** while its users are performing an exercise . It helps the users in knowing what they are doing wrong or getting right during a particular workout session and providing suggestions for improvement based on each person's individual ability level .

### Current practice and limitations
Many workout apps exist today, but they often contain generic workout plans that may not be suitable for every user. Sometimes people can be busy or live far away from gyms, so they have no other choice but to depend on remote workouts through apps or YouTube videos. These resources can be helpful, but they usually do not adapt to what the user is doing during the session. This can lead to only a surface-level understanding of exercises rather than a deeper understanding as users may lack the knowledge of how to train safely , in an effective manner, and what should be the right difficulty level for their requirements .

### Our approach (what is new)
Our approach aims to provide **guided session support** rather than only standard workout recommendations. The system will take user inputs such as target muscle group, available equipment, how long the user can exercise, and their experience level.

During the workout, the user logs each set that includes reps, weight, level of effort, and a pain flag). Based on this input, the trainer recommends the next step immediately: whether to continue, adjust weight, change reps, rest longer, switch to an easier variation, substitute an exercise, or stop if there is injury risk or if the workout seems difficult to perform.  This is like a personalized coaching guide than a plain generic plan. 
This makes the experience more easy and user friendly as it gives users a clear idea on how to proceed in their routine and make them have confidence in their skills and perform workouts in a safer and personalized manner . This is why the use of Artificial Intelligence is crucial for this project as it provides recommendations based on inputs given by different users instead of a same fixed routine for every person .


### Who benefits and impact
People who may benefit from this idea include users who are beginners in the field of exercising and want to save money and thus cannot go to the gym because it requires expensive monthly memberships, as well as the people who are very busy with their lives and do not have enough time or the opportunities to go to the gym . They mostly rely on home workouts . This is detrimental to their progress as they follow exercises and do not know if they are following it the right way from generic workout videos or tutorials found online since they cannot help during an actual live performance during a workout session . If the idea turns out to be successful, it can make performing workouts easier to follow as will be providing personalization and a more accessible and affordable option .

### Risks
Because the app will be new and might have limited ability to generate workouts, it will include some technical issues. For example, it could misinterpret the user’s logs and provide incorrect suggestions because of that . The users will have to be extremely careful about what they input and explain it clearly . Another error includes recommending exercises that do not match the target muscle groups the user wants to work on . It might listen to what users want but only provide limited exercises it knows that may or may not be helpful to the user . With time , its knwoledge and model can be improved and it can be ensured that they guess and pick up what users want properly even if the users do not organize what they want clearly .
